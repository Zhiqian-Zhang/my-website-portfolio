Mission Statement

Originally on a rigorous pre-med track, I realized that while I would enjoy practicing medicine, I was always ambivalent about my future goals. After working as a scribe in the Emergency Department, I directly witnessed both the positive and negative impacts of technology: a well-formed Emergency Medical Records (EMR) system can save a life, but a poor working interface can take time away from direct patient care. This solidified my desire to gain expertise in computer science and integrate this knowledge with my background in healthcare to increase accessibility through improved medical interfaces. What interests me the most about computer science is the ability to build the backbone of systems of the future that can bridge the gap in patient care and improve access to quality diagnoses for millions around the world. For healthcare to be universal, governments, tech agencies, and organizations have to collaborate, coordinate, and understand people for changes to occur at the patient level. This is my motivation for going into computer science: to make sure each patient is receiving the most effective and conducive care.
